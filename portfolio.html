TAMU ThinkTank Website Overhaul

September 2024 - Present

I am currently a member of the TAMU ThinkTank website development team. This is a year-long challenge to overhaul and modernize the front-end and back-end of the organization's website. During the Fall 2024 semester, we were tasked with expanding the single-page website to have multiple pages and a more responsive design using TSX and Tailwind CSS. Starting From the STEM Website

September 2024 - Present

As a member of the Aggie Coding Club, I am member of the team developing the Starting from the STEM website. This is a full-stack TSX Node.js web application intended to help students learn and hone their skills in college engineering core courses such as Calculus and Physics. During the Fall 2024 semester, I was tasked with integrating OpenAI's GPT-3.5 API in order to facilitate learning through the Feynman Technique. Currently, I am working on incorporating the chatbot into the front end using props to allow students to choose specific topics they would like to learn.



Autonomous Vehicle Simulation

September 2024 - November 2024

During the Fall 2024 semester, I worked on a team to develop an autonomous vehicle simulation using the CARLA simulator as a member of the Aggie Data Science Club. I was a member of the automobile functionality team, where I developed Python scripts to control elements of the simulation and gather raw sensor data simulating a real driving environment. In addition, I aided in the design and development of an F1 car model with sensors to be tested in the simulations.



Analog Theremin

September 2024 - November 2024

As a member of the Eta Kappa Nu (HKN) chapter at Texas A&M University, I participated in the HKN project team where we developed an analog theremin. We used a schematic to create the physical circuit using only analog components, capable of altering pitch and volume based on the user's hand proximity to the antenna sensors.
